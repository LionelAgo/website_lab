Projects Portfolio

My research portfolio includes several funded projects focusing on turbulence modeling, flow control, and machine learning applications in fluid dynamics. These projects span from fundamental research on wall-bounded turbulence to applied studies of heat transfer enhancement in engineering systems.

Through collaborations with academic and industrial partners, these projects aim to advance our understanding of complex flow phenomena and develop innovative methodologies for flow prediction and control.

ANR SOLAIRE

Role: Co-investigator

Focus: Machine learning for solar receivers

Funding for Pprime: 179,000€

This collaborative project aims to improve heat transfer in concentrated solar power systems through active control of near-wall turbulence. Our team is developing machine learning approaches to optimize control parameters for enhanced thermal performance with minimal energy penalties.

SAFRAN Aircraft Engines

Role: Project Lead

Focus: Turbulent flows in aircraft engines

Duration: 4 years

This industrial collaboration explores the exploitation of secondary flow instabilities (specifically Görtler vortices) to enhance heat transfer in aircraft engine components with minimal drag penalty. We're developing active control strategies using plasma actuators to optimize thermal management in these systems.

Future Research Directions

Looking ahead, my research will continue to push the boundaries of fluid mechanics and machine learning integration, with a particular focus on developing innovative approaches for flow control and prediction in complex engineering systems.

The following directions represent the core focus of my upcoming research initiatives, aiming to address critical challenges in energy efficiency, thermal management, and sustainable engineering.

Multi-scale Control of Wall Turbulence

Development of innovative control strategies operating simultaneously at different scales of turbulence, allowing for enhancement or limitation of heat transfer across a wide range of Reynolds numbers. These advances will have significant impact on renewable energy development and improvement of existing industrial processes using heat exchangers.

Hybrid Data-Physics Framework

Development of a methodological framework integrating physical knowledge and data-driven approaches to design optimal active control strategies. This approach will offer new possibilities for improving industrial processes, especially relevant with the recent development of additive printing that removes constraints related to complex geometries.

Adaptive Modeling for Thermofluid Systems

Design of models capable of adapting to varying flow conditions, allowing for accurate prediction and robust control in variable industrial environments. These adaptive models will pave the way for intelligent control systems to optimize heat exchanger performance in real-time.

Understanding and Controlling Wall-Bounded Turbulence

When any body moves through a fluid or when fluid flows through pipes, a boundary layer forms where various families of turbulent structures develop, significantly impacting both drag and heat transfer.

Drag Reduction in Transport

Heat Transfer Applications

These eddies play a major role in both skin-friction drag and heat transfer. As velocity increases further, new families of turbulent structures emerge and their characteristics evolve, often being influenced by wall properties and geometry. This evolution renders the flow increasingly complex and challenging to predict or control.

Drag Reduction Research

For transport applications—aircraft, ships, and pipelines—drag reduction is essential, as turbulence can increase friction by factors of 10-100 compared to laminar conditions, significantly affecting energy consumption and emissions.

Heat Transfer Control

Heat transfer is approached with dual objectives: for renewable energy harvesting (CSP plants), heat transfer must be significantly enhanced, while in engines and industrial equipment, it must be carefully regulated within optimal operating ranges.

My research is motivated by the critical need to address climate change through improved understanding and control of these wall-bounded turbulent flows. A synergistic approach is employed in my research, wherein traditional fluid mechanics is integrated with advanced machine learning techniques. Through the development of innovative control strategies targeting these boundary layer structures, significant improvements in energy efficiency and sustainability can be achieved across diverse engineering applications.

Research Interests

My research focuses on understanding the fundamental physics of turbulent flows, particularly wall-bounded turbulence, which is omnipresent in engineering applications and natural phenomena. I develop new methodologies to analyze, model, and control these complex systems with the ultimate goal of improving energy efficiency and sustainability.

Wall-bounded Turbulence

I investigate the multi-scale dynamics of turbulent flows, particularly focusing on how large-scale outer structures interact with and modulate small-scale near-wall turbulence. My work has pioneered new techniques for scale separation, challenging traditional paradigms like the quasi-steady hypothesis by revealing complex asymmetric responses and non-linear interactions. These findings are critical for understanding friction and heat transfer at high Reynolds numbers.

Learn More

Drag Reduction & Heat Transfer

My research explores innovative flow control techniques, particularly using spanwise wall oscillations to manipulate near-wall turbulence. Recently, I've achieved a significant breakthrough by demonstrating preferential enhancement of heat transfer over drag, effectively breaking the Reynolds analogy. This work has profound implications for thermal management in various engineering systems, potentially leading to more efficient heat exchangers with minimal energy penalties.

Learn More

Machine Learning in Fluid Dynamics

I develop and apply advanced machine learning techniques to extract physical insights from complex turbulent flow data. My work with auto-encoders and other data-driven methods has enabled the identification of coherent structures and the creation of reduced-order models for predicting flow behavior. This research represents a paradigm shift from traditional sequential modeling approaches to an iterative methodology that couples data-driven modeling with optimization of control strategies.

Advanced Methodologies

High-Fidelity DNS

Using direct numerical simulations to obtain detailed data on wall turbulence, allowing in-depth analysis of turbulent structures and their interactions.

Advanced Statistical Analysis

Employing sophisticated statistical methods, including joint and conditional PDF analysis, to quantify relationships between different scales and flow properties.

Bimodal Empirical Decomposition

Utilizing data-driven filtering methods to separate large and small-scale fluctuations and study their interactions in turbulent flows.

Machine Learning Techniques

Developing and applying machine learning algorithms such as auto-encoders and symbolic regression to extract key information from vast simulation datasets.
